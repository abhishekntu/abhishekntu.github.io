FPGA Virtualization

Emerging programmable SoC devices tightly couple high performance ARM processors with reconfigurable fabrics such as FPGAs. One such example is the Xilinx Zynq. The aim of my research is to investigate mechanisms for FPGA Virtualization specifically in programmable SoC devices, where FPGA can be used as a shared resource among tasks running on a general purpose processor.

Coarse Grained Overlay Architectures

Design productivity and long compilation times are major issues preventing the mainstream adoption of FPGAs in general purpose computing. Several coarse grained overlay architectures have emerged to tackle these challenges, but at the cost of increased area and performance overheads. My research is mainly focused on exploring techniques for developing area and performance efficient overlays.
